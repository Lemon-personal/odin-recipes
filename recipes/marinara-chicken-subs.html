Marinara Chicken Subs

Description

These marinara chicken subs are an easy, crowd-pleasing meal made with tender slow-cooked chicken simmered in rich marinara sauceâ€”all in the Crockpot. Piled onto toasted sub rolls and topped with melty cheeses, theyre perfect for game day, busy weeknights, or feeding a hungry group.

Ingredients

  • 3 pounds boneless skinless chicken breasts, or more to taste
  • 1 1/2 (32-ounce) jars marinara sauce
  • 2 teaspoons onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon Italian seasoning
  • 2 tablespoons minced garlic
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 3/4 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese, plus more for serving
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 8 slices provolone cheese
  • 8 sub rolls
  • 4 tablespoons butter, melted
  • chopped fresh parsley, for garnish

Directions

  1. Combine chicken, marinara sauce, Italian seasoning, garlic, salt, and pepper in a crockpot. Cook on Low for 4 to 6 hours or on High for 2 to 3 hours.
  2. Turn off crockpot and transfer chicken to a cutting board. Shred chicken into bite sized pieces using two forks or a hand mixer and return to the crockpot. Stir in Parmesan cheese.
  3. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C). Brush the inside of each sub roll with butter and toast in the preheated oven until golden brown.
  4. Cover the inside of each toasted bun with shredded mozzarella cheese, spoon chicken mixture into the bun and top with a slice of provolone cheese. Set buns on a baking sheet and return to the oven for 3 to 4 minutes until cheese is melted.
  5. Garnish sandwiches with chopped fresh parsley and freshly grated Parmesan cheese, if desired.
